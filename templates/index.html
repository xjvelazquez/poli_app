History

San Diego Local Government

City Council

  • 9 members elected by district (Districts 1–9)
  • 4-year terms; 2-term limit
  • Sets policy, approves the budget, passes ordinances

Mayor

Directly elected citywide — strong-mayor system since 2006.

  • Chief executive; manages day-to-day city operations
  • Proposes the annual budget
  • Has veto power over Council legislation
  • Current mayor: Todd Gloria (elected 2020, re-elected 2024)

City Attorney

Independently elected citywide. Provides legal counsel to the city and prosecutes misdemeanors.

City Auditor

Independently elected. Conducts performance and financial audits of city operations.

City Chief Operating Officer

Appointed by the mayor. Oversees city departments operationally.

San Diego County Board of Supervisors

(Separate from the City)

  • 5 members, each representing a county district
  • Provides public health, courts, elections, and social services across the entire county
Key Facts
  • California's 2nd-largest city; 8th largest in the US
  • 18 official city departments (police, fire-rescue, parks, public utilities, etc.)
  • Annual city budget: ~$5–6 billion
  • San Diego Unified School District governed separately by its own elected board
